You’ve reached Tünel Square—home to a small transportation miracle for people who do not enjoy surprise leg day.

What you’re seeing:
A lively junction where the historic Tünel funicular begins/ends and İstiklal’s vibe starts to build.

Why this stop matters:
It’s the ‘gateway’ moment: from port-side Galata up into Beyoğlu’s culture-and-café world.

Historical snapshot:
The Tünel opened in 1875, created to solve the steep hill between Karaköy and Beyoğlu.

The human story:
Imagine 19th-century commuters stepping into a brand-new underground railway like it’s science fiction—and then just… going to work.

Look closer (Engineering):
Short route, huge impact. Sometimes the best infrastructure is the kind you forget because it simply works.

Cheap eats / insider tip:
If you’re tired later, remember: this is a fun way to ride down without turning your knees into spaghetti.

Fun facts:
• The Tünel (1875) is among the world’s oldest underground urban railways.
• It solves a problem: this hill is steep.

If you remember one thing about this place, remember this:
In Istanbul, even a short ride can be historic.
